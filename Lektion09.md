### Concepts & Grammar
* **Topic:** The lesson focuses on the "Flohmarkt" (flea market) culture in Germany, leisure activities, and coordinating plans with friends.
* **Compound Nouns (Komposita):** German often combines two nouns to create a new one. 
    * **Rule:** The article and the plural form of the new word are always determined by the **second noun** (the "Head" noun).
    * *Example:* `der Winter` + `die Jacke` = `die Winterjacke` (The winter jacket).
* **Preposition "unter":** In this context, it means "among" or "between" (e.g., *unter Freunden* - among friends).

## [06:24](https://youtube.com/watch?v=IARyId4N_sA&t=384s) ## Grammar: Sentence Connectors (Position 0)
### The "Nullposition" Rule
German has specific conjunctions (connectors) that connect two main clauses but **do not occupy a numbered position** in the sentence. They are essentially at "Position 0."

* **The 5 Main Connectors (ADUSO):**
    1. **aber** (but)
    2. **denn** (because/for)
    3. **und** (and)
    4. **sondern** (but rather - not covered in detail here)
    5. **oder** (or)

### Sentence Structure [07:11](https://youtube.com/watch?v=IARyId4N_sA&t=431s)
When using these, the next word (usually the subject) is Position 1, and the conjugated verb remains in Position 2.
* *Example (und):* "Ich studiere **und** ich jobbe ein bisschen." (I study and I work a bit.)
* *Example (denn):* "Ich lerne Deutsch, **denn** ich m√∂chte in Wien studieren." (I learn German because I want to study in Vienna.) [12:35](https://youtube.com/watch?v=IARyId4N_sA&t=755s)
* *Example (aber):* "Ich war bei Microsoft, **aber** der Job war nichts f√ºr mich." (I was at Microsoft, but the job wasn't for me.) [10:13](https://youtube.com/watch?v=IARyId4N_sA&t=613s)

## [15:40](https://youtube.com/watch?v=9Iq6cRcyIJ4&t=940s) ## Grammar: Compound Nouns (Komposita)
### Rules
In German, two or more nouns can be joined to form a single word. 
1. The **article** of the new word is always the article of the **last** noun.
2. The **plural** form also follows the last noun.

| First Word | Second Word | Compound Noun | Meaning |

### Usage
* **wollen:** To want (expresses intent or a firm plan).
* **m√∂gen:** To like (expresses a preference).

> üí° **Tip:** In German modal verbs, the 1st person (**ich**) and 3rd person singular (**er/sie/es**) always have the **same** conjugation. [21:26](https://youtube.com/watch?v=9Iq6cRcyIJ4&t=1286s)

## Cultural Context
* **Mauerpark Flohmarkt:** One of the most famous flea markets in Berlin. It is known for its karaoke and vibrant atmosphere, attracting thousands every Sunday.
* **Sunday Closing:** Most shops and markets in Germany are closed on Sundays ("Sonntagsruhe"). Flea markets are a rare exception where commerce happens on a Sunday.
* **Tipping:** While not as mandatory as in some countries, it is common in Germany to round up the bill or leave a small tip (approx. 5-10%) in restaurants.
