| Scenario | Question/Statement | Response | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Positive Question | Wohnst du in Berlin? | Ja / Nein | Standard yes/no. |
| Negative Question | Wohnst du **nicht** in Berlin? | **Doch!** | Yes, I actually *do* live there. |

> [!TIP]

## [10:39](https://youtube.com/watch?v=qgh_rpYePjU&t=639s) Grammar: Dative Prepositions (in & auf)
This section covers two essential two-way prepositions used here to describe location (answering the question **"Wo?"** - Where?).

### 1. The Prepositions
* **in:** Inside/In.
* **auf:** On top of (surface contact).

### 2. Dative Article Changes
When answering "Wo?", the articles change in the Dative case:
| Gender | Nominative | Dative | Short Form |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Maskulin** | der | **dem** | in + dem = **im** |
| **Neutral** | das | **dem** | in + dem = **im** |
| **Feminin** | die | **der** | - |

## Cultural Context
* **WG (Wohngemeinschaft):** Flat-sharing is extremely common in Germany, not just for students but also for young professionals. It's interesting to note the dialogue mentions a "WG in the village," which is less common but possible.
* **Housing Adverts:** Germans often look for apartments through "Anzeigen" (ads). When Paul says "Ich rufe an," he is following a standard procedure of calling the landlord or current tenant.
* **Italian Design:** In Germany, Italian furniture and design are often associated with high quality and luxury ("Alles ist aus Italien").
