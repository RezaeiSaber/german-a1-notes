## Grammar: Verb Position (Position 2) [11:18](https://youtube.com/watch?v=w4aGKukeqvk&t=678s)

In a standard German statement (not a question or command), the **conjugated verb** MUST be in **Position 2**.

### The Rule
1. The subject (e.g., *ich, wir*) usually sits in Position 1.
2. However, you can move another element (like an adverb for emphasis) to Position 1.
3. If Position 1 is occupied by something else, the subject must move to **Position 3** so the verb stays in **Position 2**.

### Examples:
* **Normal:** "Ich (**Pos 1**) trinke (**Pos 2**) oft Tee."
* **Emphasized:** "Oft (**Pos 1**) trinke (**Pos 2**) ich (**Pos 3**) Tee." [12:10](https://youtube.com/watch?v=w4aGKukeqvk&t=730s)

## The Imperative (Commands) [16:04](https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y3CsOmkXSrM&t=964s)

The imperative is used to give orders, advice, or suggestions.

### 1. Informal Command (du-form)
To form a command for someone you call "du":
1. Start with the conjugated verb.
2. Remove the **-st** ending and the pronoun **du**.
* *Example:* **mach** (from *du machst*) ‚Üí **Mach Sport!** (Do sports!) [16:50](https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y3CsOmkXSrM&t=1010s)
* *Example:* **komm** (from *du kommst*) ‚Üí **Komm nach Deutschland!** [17:48](https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y3CsOmkXSrM&t=1068s)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Irregular:** The imperative of **essen** for "du" is **Iss!** [33:10](https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y3CsOmkXSrM&t=1990s)

### 2. Formal Command (Sie-form)
To form a command for someone you call "Sie":
1. Move the verb to the beginning of the sentence.
2. Keep the pronoun **Sie** immediately after the verb.
* *Example:* **Trinken Sie** viel Wasser! (Drink plenty of water!) [25:02](https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y3CsOmkXSrM&t=1502s)
* *Example:* **Essen Sie** gesund! (Eat healthily!) [27:23](https://youtube.com/watch?v=Y3CsOmkXSrM&t=1643s)
