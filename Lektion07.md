The **Perfekt** is the most common way to speak about the past in German.

### üí° The Formula
**[Auxiliary Verb: haben or sein] + [Partizip II (Past Participle)]**

1.  **Auxiliary Verb**: Positioned in the 2nd place of the sentence. It is conjugated to match the subject.
2.  **Partizip II**: Placed at the very end of the sentence. It does not change.

### üõ†Ô∏è Forming Partizip II (Regular Verbs) [00:14:43](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F-zsjzBnHmU&t=883s)
For regular (weak) verbs: **ge- + [Verb Stem] + -t**
* *machen* -> **gemacht**
* *lernen* -> **gelernt**
* *leben* -> **gelebt**

### ‚ùì Choosing Between "haben" and "sein" [00:18:10](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F-zsjzBnHmU&t=1090s)
* **sein**: Used for verbs showing **movement** (A to B) or a **change of state**.
    * *gehen* -> `Ich bin gegangen.` (I went). [00:19:32](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F-zsjzBnHmU&t=1172s)
    * *fahren* -> `Ich bin gefahren.` (I drove). [00:21:06](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F-zsjzBnHmU&t=1266s)
* **haben**: Used for most other verbs, especially those that take a direct object.
    * *h√∂ren* -> `Er hat Musik geh√∂rt.` (He listened to music). [00:21:40](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=F-zsjzBnHmU&t=1300s)

## Cultural Context
* **Gymnasium & Abitur:** In Germany, a *Gymnasium* is the highest track of secondary school, and the *Abitur* is the final exam required to enter university.
* **Years:** For years before 2000, Germans say the hundreds (e.g., 1988 = *neunzehnhundertachtundachtzig*). For years after 2000, they say the full number (e.g., 2010 = *zweitausendzehn*). [08:58](https://youtube.com/watch?v=X1BmH031jzw&t=538s)
