# German A1 Lesson Notes: Articles, Negation, and Formal Farewells üìù‚ú®

## Articles and Negation [01:14](https://youtube.com/watch?v=G3novmAiNas&t=74s)

German nouns are categorized by three genders, each with a definite (the), indefinite (a/an), and negative (none/not a) article.

### üéØ Grammar: Article Table
| Gender | Definite (the) | Indefinite (a/an) | Negative (not a) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masculine** | **der** Bus | **ein** Bus | **kein** Bus |
| **Neuter** | **das** Taxi | **ein** Taxi | **kein** Taxi |
| **Feminine** | **die** Bluse | **eine** Bluse | **keine** Bluse |

### üí° Key Concepts:
* **Bestimmt (Definite):** Used for a specific, known object (e.g., *Das ist der Bus* - That is *the* specific bus we know).
* **Unbestimmt (Indefinite):** Used for any general object (e.g., *Das ist ein Bus* - This is *a* bus).
* **Negation (Negationsartikel):** Used to say something "is not a" noun. 
    * *Example:* "Das ist **kein** Laptop, das ist ein MacBook." [05:21](https://youtube.com/watch?v=G3novmAiNas&t=321s)

---

## Sentence Structure: Yes/No Questions [16:38](https://youtube.com/watch?v=G3novmAiNas&t=998s)

In the previous lesson, we learned that the verb sits in Position 2. For **Yes/No Questions**, the verb moves to **Position 1**.

1.  **Statement:** `Du` (1) `sprichst` (2) `Deutsch` (3).
2.  **Question:** `Sprichst` (1) `du` (2) `Deutsch` (3)?

> ‚ú® **Key Takeaway:** If there is no question word (like *Wie* or *Wo*), start the sentence with the conjugated verb to ask a question. [17:10](https://youtube.com/watch?v=G3novmAiNas&t=1030s)

## Cultural Context
* **Formal vs. Informal:** Always use **"Wie geht es Ihnen?"** (formal) with people you don't know well or in professional settings. Use **"Wie geht's?"** (informal) for friends.
* **Tipping/Payment:** In food stalls, the phrase **"Das macht..."** is the standard way a seller tells you the total price.
* **Regionalisms:** Germany has many dialects and regional greetings (like **Gr√ºezi**), which you will often encounter in listening exercises.
