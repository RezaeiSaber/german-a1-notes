## Verb Conjugation: Regular Verbs [00:57](https://youtube.com/watch?v=RcjVcv5j79E&t=57s)

In German, most verbs follow a regular pattern when conjugated. To find the "stem" of a verb, you usually remove the **-en** ending.

### ðŸŽ¯ Conjugation Rule
Add these endings to the verb stem:
- **ich**: -e
- **du**: -st
- **er/sie/es**: -t
- **wir**: -en
- **ihr**: -t
- **sie/Sie**: -en

### Key Verbs in this Lesson:
1.  **kommen** (to come): Used to state origin (*aus*). [03:08](https://youtube.com/watch?v=RcjVcv5j79E&t=188s)
2.  **wohnen** (to live/reside): Used to state residence (*in*). [09:19](https://youtube.com/watch?v=RcjVcv5j79E&t=559s)

## Grammar: Sentence Structure (Satzbau) [20:14](https://youtube.com/watch?v=rmNNn2kJ08o&t=1214s)

In a standard German statement, the **conjugated verb always sits in the second position**.

1.  **Position 1:** Subject or other emphasis.
2.  **Position 2:** Verb.
3.  **Position 3:** Remaining information.

* *Example:* `Ich` (1) `mache` (2) `gern Sport` (3).
* *Example:* `Gern` (1) `mache` (2) `ich Sport` (3).

âš ï¸ **Warning:** The subject must stay next to the verb (either in position 1 or 3). [21:03](https://youtube.com/watch?v=rmNNn2kJ08o&t=1263s)

## Capitalization Rules (GroÃŸschreibung) [26:19](https://youtube.com/watch?v=rmNNn2kJ08o&t=1579s)

German is unique because it capitalizes all nouns.

* **Always Capitalize:**
    * The first word of a sentence.
    * Proper names (Hamed, Nina, Berlin).
    * **All nouns** (any word that has an article like *der/die/das*).
* **Write in Lowercase:**
    * Verbs, adjectives, and adverbs (unless they start a sentence).

## Cultural Context
* **Formal vs. Informal:** The instructor highlights the strict distinction between **"du"** (friends, family, peers) and **"Sie"** (strangers, superiors, formal settings). Mixing these up can be seen as impolite in Germany.
* **Modal Particles:** Words like **"denn"** or **"doch"** are used to color the language and express emotion or emphasis without changing the basic meaning of the sentence.
